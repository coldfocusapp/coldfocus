Account & Data Deletion Request

Cold Focus mobile app · Last updated: July 3, 2026

This page explains how to delete your account and the associated data in the Cold Focus app. Cold Focus is provided by the developer Hasan Burak Erdin (Coldfocusapp).

In short: Cold Focus is an offline-first app, and most of your data stays only on your device. Uninstalling the app deletes all local data on the device. If you used cloud sync, you can also delete your server-side data using the methods below.

1. Deleting on-device (local) data

Your focus session records, progress state and preferences are kept primarily on your device. To delete them:

  1. Uninstall the app from your phone, or
  2. Go to Android Settings → Apps → Cold Focus → Storage → Clear Data.

This permanently deletes all local data on the device.

2. Deleting your cloud account and server data

If you enabled cloud sync, you can request deletion of the anonymous account identifier, session summaries and profile information held on our servers (Supabase).

How to request

Send a message to coldfocusapp@gmail.com with the subject line "Account Deletion". So that we can verify your request, please write from the Google / Apple email address you linked to your account if possible, or include the account identifier shown on the in-app Settings screen.

→ Create a deletion request email

3. What data is deleted

  • Your anonymous account identifier.
  • Your focus session summaries (length, outcome, difficulty, violation count, local date/time).
  • Your profile information (premium status, time zone, identity level, streak, notification token).

Note: An active subscription is managed by the relevant store (Google Play / App Store); deleting your account does not automatically cancel the subscription. You must cancel your subscription separately from the store's subscription settings. Also, due to legal obligations, certain transaction/payment records may be retained on the store's side.

4. Timing

After we receive your request, we verify your identity and delete your cloud data within 30 days at the latest. Authentication logs kept for security purposes and containing IP addresses are already deleted automatically within 30 days.
